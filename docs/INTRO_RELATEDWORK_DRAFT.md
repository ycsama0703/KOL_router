## 0. Abstract (draft — the north star; every section must serve this)

LLM-based agents are increasingly entrusted with financial trading and event forecasting, and they consume information at a scale and speed no human analyst can match. Yet the fastest, most time-sensitive input available to them — the social-media stream — is used least well. The agents that ingest it at all pour the raw feed in and aggregate sentiment after the crowd has already reacted, never asking the two questions that decide a narrative's value: *which account originated it*, and *how far will it travel*. The moment that matters is origination — before any resharing or popularity is observable, and before the signal, which decays within seconds, is gone. Naively handing that stream to an LLM fails twice over: it is too slow at firehose volume, and, counter-intuitively, a larger raw pool makes the model's selection *worse*, not better.

We show that financial key-opinion-leader streams contain a stable, identity-specific **lead-lag originator structure**: certain accounts systematically originate narratives that others later adopt, and this structure is measurable at origination time from an account's prior history, deconfounded from mere posting-hour or timezone effects. We encode it as a directed lead-lag graph and rank freshly-originated narratives by their eventual follower-weighted reach with a listwise ranker that reads *zero text* — the **Lead-Lag Router (LLR)**. Reading no text and costing effectively nothing per item, LLR nonetheless outranks every state-of-the-art text encoder and every full LLM we test, at orders-of-magnitude lower latency and cost. Controlled ablations trace the result to the origination topology itself — an identity-shuffled graph erases the gain — rather than to model capacity or added features. Finally, the same router doubles as a budgeted triage stage that feeds a high-value shortlist to downstream reasoning, and as an early-event detector on which text encoders and LLMs fare no better than chance.

## 1. Introduction (draft)

**P0 — The agentic trading wave and its social media gap.** LLM-based trading agent frameworks have proliferated rapidly [FinAgent 2024; TradingGPT 2023; MarketSenseAI 2024; FinMem 2023; FINCON 2024; TradExpert 2024], driven by a compelling premise: agents can execute the full intelligence pipeline — data ingestion, feature construction, model building, and order generation — far faster than any human analyst, absorbing information at a scale and speed no individual expert can match. Yet a conspicuous gap persists. A recent survey of LLM trading agents [Ding et al. 2024] finds that incorporating social-media data into trading agents remains "understudied," citing SEP [Koa et al. 2024] as the only reviewed system that ingests real-time social media; the rare agents that do (e.g., TradingAgents [Xiao et al. 2024]) aggregate social-media sentiment at the date level without attributing narratives to their *originating accounts*. This is not a deliberate design choice — it reflects the absence of a workable entry point for a data source that is structurally different from institutional news: high-volume, identity-heterogeneous, and acutely time-sensitive.

**P0.1 — The same gap spans event forecasting, where timing is even more decisive.** Social media is not merely a trading input; in *event-prediction* settings — forecasting whether a real-world event will occur, as traded on prediction markets or benchmarked by forecasting systems — a freshly-originated narrative is often the *first observable evidence* that an event is materializing, and the value of that evidence collapses the instant the crowd reacts. Yet the event-prediction literature exhibits the identical blind spot. Social-media event-prediction models either encode the post stream by *content-salience attention* — weighting posts by textual influence but with no notion of which account posted, or posted first [StockNet 2018; MAN-SF, Sawhney 2020], detect *firm-mention volume spikes after the crowd has already reacted* [first-story detection: Petrović 2010; Tafti 2016], weight by static follower count — which is actively misleading, since 56% of retail finfluencers are "antiskilled" (advice yielding −2.3% monthly abnormal returns) yet attract *larger* followings [Kakhbod et al. 2023] — or, at best, weight by the *consistency of an account's past predictions* rather than its *origination timing* [Zhou 2025]. The one line that does ask "who originated this" — rumor-source detection — performs *retrospective* forensic inference on a *completed* cascade, never as a *forward* routing filter [survey: 2021]. Meanwhile, LLM-based forecasting systems do not consume social streams at all: they retrieve *news articles* [Halawi 2024] or rely on the models' *parametric knowledge* [Schoenegger 2024]. Across both trading and forecasting, an origination-aware, point-in-time routing filter for social streams is a genuine empty cell.

**P1 — Two failure modes of naïve social media ingestion.** When agent systems incorporate social streams without a dedicated filtering stage, two compounding failures arise. (i) *Latency*: the market impact of a market-moving post is compressed into a few seconds — volatility spikes within one second, directional impact lasts on the order of two seconds, and the reaction dissipates to baseline within roughly ten [Scharnowski 2026]; by the time a raw pool is aggregated and routed through an LLM, the window has closed. (ii) *Dilution*: feeding a large candidate pool to an LLM degrades its selection quality rather than improving it. In our experiments, a large LLM handed the full stream achieves a follower-weighted reach-capture of 0.455; the same LLM, given only a cheap pre-filtered shortlist, reaches 0.636 — a 40% relative gain (§4). Opposing signals cancel, the model defaults to generic conclusions, and large volumes of data, compute, and time are wasted. The raw information-acquisition power of agent systems thus actively undermines their usefulness on time-critical social signals.

**P2 — The missing origination gate.** What the pipeline needs is a gate that fires *at the moment of narrative origination* — before any resharing, early-adopter signal, or popularity is observable — ranks incoming narratives by their eventual follower-weighted reach, and delivers only a high-signal shortlist to the expensive downstream model. This is a *point-in-time (PIT) early-ranking* problem under strict latency and token budgets. No existing tool solves it directly: cascade models (DeepCas [Li 2017]; DeepHawkes [Cao 2017]) require an early-adopter observation window; content models (BERT, FinBERT, E5, full LLMs) read post text and are both costly and — as we show — less accurate than our structure-only approach; CasMS [Zhou 2024], the only zero-observation cascade model, still depends on message text and a static adopter-propensity signal.

**P3 — Our discovery.** We show that financial KOL streams contain a stable, identity-specific **lead-lag originator structure**: certain accounts systematically *originate* narratives before others *adopt* them, and this structure is measurable point-in-time from pre-period posting history, deconfounded against posting-hour and timezone. The structure is strong enough that a **simple linear model over it — reading zero text — already outranks every text encoder (NDCG@3 0.745 vs. ≤0.732) and every full LLM tested (DeepSeek 0.623; local 7–8B models 0.53–0.55)**, at ~0.002 ms and 0 tokens per item. Encoding the structure as a directed lead-lag graph and training a listwise cross-entropy GBDT — our **Lead-Lag Router (LLR)** — lifts performance to NDCG@3 0.812 with a statistically significant increment over a strong context baseline (+0.0072 NDCG, pooled, 90% CI). Replacing the real graph with an identity-shuffled control degrades performance by −0.011 NDCG (95% CI), confirming the gain comes from genuine origination-network topology rather than added model capacity.

**P4 — Contributions.**
1. **An originator-structure discovery + verification.** We define a deconfounded, PIT lead-lag originator trait (scalar O_k + graph net-degree g_net) for financial cashtag KOLs and verify it is a real, identity-specific signal via shuffle controls.
2. **A cheap structure router that beats text SOTA.** A linear/tree ranker over the structure (zero text) matches or beats SOTA text encoders and full LLMs on pre-popularity narrative-reach ranking at 2-6 orders of magnitude lower latency/cost.
3. **Rigorous ablations + prior-art comparison.** We attribute the result (listwise objective is the engine; g_net not network-centrality; structure significant only when graph-encoded) and show — by re-implementing them — that prior account signals (Romero influence, Yamada source-spreader, Zhou prediction-consistency) are either subsumed by context or add nothing, while our structure adds significantly.
4. **An application.** The router doubles as an early-breakout / event-prediction layer for agentic systems (a budgeted triage stage before expensive LLM reasoning).

**P5 — Roadmap.** Section 2 positions us against prior work; Section 3 defines the data, structure, and router; Section 4 reports the main table; Section 5 the ablations; Section 6 the application.

## 2. Related Work (draft, organized by the audit)

### 2.1 Early / pre-popularity cascade prediction
Cascade-size models such as **DeepCas [Li 2017]** and **DeepHawkes [Cao 2017]** predict future diffusion from an *observed early cascade graph* (random-walk paths or Hawkes intensities over the first hours of adopters). They are accurate but structurally require an observation window and thus cannot fire at origination. **CasMS [Zhou 2024]** is, to our knowledge, the only model with an explicit zero-observation "message-generation" stage; however it relies on message text (BERT) plus an adopter-side retweet-propensity signal over a static social graph. We differ on all of: **zero text**, an **originator (not adopter) lead-lag trait with posting-hour deconfounding**, a **narrative-frame unit with follower-weighted reach** (not single-message retweet count), and a structure-beats-LLM cost claim. A best-effort generation-stage CasMS baseline (its zero-observation inputs — message embedding + originator node2vec graph-position — in our ranker) scores 0.695 NDCG, below text-only (0.719) and far below our structure router (0.813, +0.118); its graph-position embedding alone is weak (0.494). **[Weng 2013]** shows the *community structure* of early spread predicts meme virality (outperforming raw early-volume baselines), but on general memes, requiring early adopters, with no per-account identity trait.

### 2.2 Content-only popularity prediction
**[Stokowiec 2017]** predicts popularity from title text alone with a BiLSTM and argues deep text beats shallow text by ~15%. Our result is the counter-thesis in this domain: a zero-text structural model beats deep text encoders and LLMs. We cite this line as the content-only school our structure router overturns.

### 2.3 Influencers, opinion leaders, and origination traits
A long line measures per-account influence: **[Cha 2010]** (the million-follower fallacy — follower count ≠ influence; influence is stable across topics/time), **[Romero 2011]** (influence-passivity IP score on a directed retweet graph, predictive of URL clicks), and recently **[Yamada 2025]** (separating *stable* from *temporal* influencers via a source-spreader-vs-broker split, zero-text LightGBM / gradient-boosted trees). In the financial domain specifically, **[Kakhbod et al. 2023]** ("Finfluencers") find that 56% of retail finfluencers are *antiskilled* (their advice yields −2.3% monthly abnormal returns), yet the unskilled and antiskilled attract *more* followers and exert *more* influence on retail trading than skilled ones — so follower count is *negatively*, not merely un-, correlated with advice quality. We take this as motivation (our own extrapolation, not their claim) that audience size is an adversarial ranking signal, which is why our trait is derived from lead-lag origination timing and residualized rather than from follower count. These establish that identity-specific, structurally-derived account signals are stable and predictive. We extend this in four ways that prior work does not combine: a **pairwise lead-lag (posting-time) origination** trait rather than self-amplification or eigenvector influence; **residualization against posting-hour/timezone**; a **fresh-narrative reach-ranking** target (not account persistence or URL clicks); and the **financial cashtag** domain. We further re-implement Romero's IP score, Yamada's source-spreader, and a Zhou-style prediction-consistency signal on our data and show (Section 5) that they are subsumed by simple context features or add nothing over them, whereas our structure adds a significant increment.

### 2.4 Financial social media
**[Hentschel 2014]** descriptively characterizes cashtag tweets; **[Rakowski 2021]** links aggregate cashtag volume to investor attention and prices via a Twitter-outage natural experiment; **[Li 2025]** predicts abrupt price changes from conversation-level similarity. The closest is **[Zhou 2025]** (*Unleashing Expert Opinion from Social Media for Stock Prediction*, arXiv:2504.10078), whose dynamic *expert-tracing* algorithm filters out non-informative StockTwits posts and identifies experts — both consistently-correct *and* consistently-wrong "inverse" experts — by the *consistency of their past predictions*, using their sentiment as a signal for *stock trend / return* prediction. The distinctions are along four axes: the **trait** — origination/lead-lag *timing*, not prediction consistency; the **target** — a freshly-originated narrative's follower-weighted *reach*, not an individual account's stock-return accuracy; the **structure** — a directed account-to-account lead-lag graph, not per-account sentiment consistency; and the **evaluation** — we run an explicit structure-vs-LLM head-to-head, which their setup does not. Their signal is *who is (in)variably right*; ours is *who fires first*.

### 2.5 Event prediction and prediction markets from social signals
A parallel literature forecasts real-world events from social media, and it exhibits the same origination-agnostic pattern. Content-based movement predictors encode the daily post corpus by *content-salience attention* — weighting posts by textual "quality"/market influence, with no account-identity or first-poster variable (**[StockNet, Xu & Cohen 2018]**; **[MAN-SF, Sawhney et al. 2020]**); event/burst detectors fire on *firm-mention volume spikes* (e.g., 99th-percentile mention rates) — i.e., after the crowd has already reacted — in the first-story-detection lineage (**[Petrović 2010]**; **[Tafti et al. 2016]**), which is structurally backward-looking. The rumor-source-detection line (**[survey 2021]**) is the only body that explicitly localizes *who originated* a cascade, but it does *retrospective* forensic inference over a *completed* cascade for misinformation containment — never as a forward, point-in-time routing filter, and never for market-moving financial signals. On the forecasting side, recent LLM forecasting systems consume no social streams at all: Halawi et al. 2024 retrieve *news articles* (via news APIs, ranked by LLM-judged relevance, aggregated by a trimmed mean of forecasts), and Schoenegger et al. 2024 rely on the models' *parametric knowledge* over a Metaculus tournament. We occupy the empty cell: a *forward*, PIT, originator-first router for live financial social streams. Our event-prediction experiment (§6) instantiates this — ranking freshly-originated narratives by whether they become top-decile reach events, where every text encoder and LLM we test performs near-random while our structure router does not.

### 2.6 Primitives we build on
We detect freshly-originated frames in the spirit of streaming first-story / novelty detection **[Petrović et al. 2010]** (LSH-accelerated nearest-neighbour cosine search over the stream). Concretely, we cluster posts online by greedy cosine similarity over MiniLM sentence embeddings, represent each frame as the L2-normalised centroid of its member-post embeddings, and score a post's novelty by its maximum cosine similarity to prior frame centroids. This is an embedding-clustering construction — *not* a semantic-frame / subject-verb-object parse — and is a detection primitive, not a predictor: it carries no originator signal and is not a competitor.

## 3. One-paragraph positioning (for the abstract / intro close)

> Prior work establishes (a) stable, structurally-derived per-account influence/origination traits [Cha 2010; Romero 2011; Yamada 2025], (b) expert identification on financial social media by prediction-consistency tracing [Zhou 2025], (c) zero-observation popularity prediction from message text [Stokowiec 2017; CasMS 2024], and (d) that the community structure of early spread predicts virality better than raw early volume [Weng 2013]. We are the first to combine a **deconfounded, point-in-time lead-lag originator structure** over financial cashtag KOLs with a **zero-text lightweight router** that ranks freshly-originated narratives by future follower-weighted reach before popularity is observable — matching or beating SOTA text encoders and full LLMs at orders-of-magnitude lower cost, with the structural signal verified real by shuffle controls and shown to add significantly beyond both strong context features and re-implemented prior account signals.
